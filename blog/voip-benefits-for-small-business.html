7 Ways VoIP Phone Systems Save Small Businesses Money

Traditional landlines are costing UK small businesses far more than they realise. Here's how switching to cloud-hosted VoIP can cut your phone bill by up to 60% while unlocking features you never had before.

If you're still running your business on a traditional BT landline or a legacy PBX system, you're almost certainly overpaying — and missing out on features that your competitors may already be using. Cloud-hosted VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) has transformed business communications, and the savings are very real.

Here are seven concrete ways VoIP cuts costs for UK small businesses.

1. Lower monthly line rental

Traditional PSTN and ISDN lines charge a fixed monthly rental per physical line, often £20–£40 per line. A cloud VoIP system replaces those physical lines entirely. You pay a per-user subscription (typically £8–£18/month depending on features) and your broadband does the rest. For a business with 5 phone users, that's often a saving of £100+ per month before you've even made a call.

2. Near-zero international call costs

Traditional landlines charge per-minute rates for international calls that can reach £0.50–£2.00/minute for some destinations. VoIP routes calls over the internet, reducing international call costs by up to 90%. If you call overseas clients, partners, or suppliers regularly, this saving alone can pay for your entire VoIP subscription.

3. No expensive hardware to maintain

A legacy PBX system requires physical on-site hardware — a box that costs thousands to install, requires specialist engineers to maintain, and becomes obsolete. Cloud VoIP is maintained entirely by the provider. You never need to call an engineer for a firmware update or a hardware fault. Upgrade costs are zero.

4. Add or remove users instantly

Adding a new employee to a traditional phone system means ordering a new physical line (which can take weeks and cost installation fees). With VoIP, a new user can be set up in minutes with no hardware and no line order. You only pay for the users you have — scaling down is just as easy, and you'll never pay for unused capacity.

5. Remote and hybrid working at no extra cost

Your VoIP number works anywhere with an internet connection — in the office, at home, or on a mobile app. Staff can take their work number with them wherever they go, answering and making calls as if they were at their desk. There is no extra cost to set this up, and you avoid the need for expensive call-forwarding from desk phones to mobiles.

6. Enterprise features included as standard

Features that would cost thousands on a legacy PBX — auto-attendant, call queuing, voicemail-to-email, call recording, conference bridging, CRM integration — come included in KomTek's VoIP plans at no additional cost. You get an enterprise-grade phone system without the enterprise price tag.

7. One bill, no surprises

Traditional phone systems often come with a confusing mix of line rental, maintenance contracts, call charges, and hardware leasing. A VoIP subscription consolidates everything into one predictable monthly fee, making budgeting simpler and eliminating bill-shock.

Frequently asked questions

How much can a small business save by switching to VoIP?

Most UK small businesses save between 40% and 60% on their monthly phone bills. International call costs can be reduced by up to 90% compared with traditional landline rates.

Do I need special equipment to use VoIP?

No. You can use a desktop app, mobile app, or a VoIP handset. Any existing broadband connection will work — we recommend at least 1 Mbps per concurrent call.

Is VoIP reliable enough for business use?

KomTek's VoIP system provides a 99.999% uptime guarantee — equivalent to less than 6 minutes of downtime per year. Calls are automatically rerouted if any issue arises, keeping your business connected at all times.
